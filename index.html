Global food waste poses a substantial challenge for societies, encompassing economic, social, and environmental dimensions. The wasteful disposal of food results in a squandering of critical resources such as land and water, which are increasingly scarce as the global population grows. This has direct economic repercussions, impacting everyone from farmers and food distributors to consumers who bear the financial burden of discarded food. Moreover, the ethical concerns associated with wasting food in a world where many suffer from hunger underscore the need for a more equitable distribution of resources. The environmental impact of food waste is significant, contributing to greenhouse gas emissions through methane production in landfills and exacerbating climate change. Addressing food waste is not only an ethical responsibility but also a strategic approach to mitigating the environmental consequences associated with food production. Additionally, the social and cultural implications of reevaluating societal norms surrounding food consumption and disposal are integral to understanding and addressing this issue. Effective policies and regulations to combat food waste necessitate a comprehensive understanding of its scale and impact, emphasizing the importance of studying and mitigating this global challenge.

I created this website for people to see just how much food was being wasted around the world, and which countries/regions waste more than others.
